1. Introduction

At Jeevadhara, we are committed to protecting the privacy of our users and contributors. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how it is used, and the rights you have in connection with your data.

2. Information We Collect

Jeevadhara does not, by default, collect or store personal health information. Any patient or medical data used within the project must be fully anonymized before being processed. When you interact with our website or community spaces, we may collect limited technical information, such as browser type, device information, and usage statistics, to improve functionality and ensure security. If you voluntarily contribute to the project, for example through GitHub or community forums, you may provide information such as your name, email address, or username.

3. Use of Information

The information we collect is used solely to operate, maintain, and improve Jeevadhara and its related services. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information under any circumstances.

4. Data Handling and Security

Jeevadhara is designed with a privacy-first approach. Any health-related data that may be used for research or development purposes must be anonymized and handled in compliance with applicable data protection laws. Our systems support privacy-preserving technologies, including federated learning, which allow models to improve without requiring raw data to leave local environments. While we take reasonable measures to secure the information processed through our services, contributors and users remain responsible for ensuring that the data they provide complies with relevant laws such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), and any applicable Indian regulations.
